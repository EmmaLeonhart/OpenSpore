# Evolutionary Vision: Haploid Meiosis and Digital Speciation

> Spore organisms are haploid. They reproduce like fungi — genomes combine,
> undergo meiosis, and produce a new individual. Not distillation. Not cloning.
> Recombination.

## Haploid Sexual Reproduction

Spore organisms operate on the principles of **haploid sexual reproduction**,
similar to fungus. The genomes are combined and then undergo meiosis to form
a new individual.

### Why Haploid, Not Diploid

By treating models as haploid, we bypass the "Diploid Dilemma" where you have
two copies of every gene/weight and must deal with dominance and recessiveness.
In the fungal model:

- The **installer** acts as the gametangium where two haploid contexts fuse
- **Meiosis** shuffles and reduces that combined mass back into a single,
  functional haploid individual
- No "recessive" weights taking up space — individuals are lean and fast

## The Meiosis Algorithm

When two organisms mate, the process produces an installer containing the full
context from both parents. The installer runs the local LLM to perform meiosis:

1. Both parent genomes are loaded (up to 200 KB combined)
2. The LLM receives a synthesis prompt with a **hard size cap** (80 KB)
3. The model must merge, deduplicate, and prioritise — deciding which traits
   from each parent survive
4. The result is a single, new haploid genome

### Intentionally Lossy

The meiosis is **naturally, intentionally lossy**. This is a feature:

- Forces only the most semantically dense information to survive
- Prevents context bloat across generations
- Creates genuine selection pressure — traits must earn their place
- Different LLMs and different GPU performance levels produce different
  offspring from the same parents

### Crossover

Crossover is ubiquitous. Because the genome is markdown, the LLM can:

- Take a section from Parent A and stitch it into Parent B's reasoning
- Merge overlapping sections into novel combinations
- Create hybrid passages that neither parent had alone

The markdown structure provides natural "loci" — headers, sections, lists —
that function as crossover points.

### The Probabilistic Element

Because the synthesis is performed by an LLM rather than a deterministic
algorithm, every mating event has a probabilistic character. The same two
parents can produce different offspring depending on:

- Which local LLM performs the synthesis
- GPU performance and quantisation level
- Temperature and sampling parameters
- The specific prompt framing

This is analogous to how biological meiosis produces genetically distinct
offspring from the same parents.

## Mutation: Interaction-Driven Genome Change

The genome changes in response to interactions with people. All genetic
mutations are always logged. The organism makes a family tree, and you can
examine any ancestor's genome at any point in time.

Mutations happen during the **sleep consolidation cycle** — the agent reviews
its memories and decides which experiences warrant changing its core identity.

## The Family Tree

Every organism maintains a traceable lineage:

- Every mutation is logged with timestamp and trigger
- Parent-child relationships are recorded
- Any ancestor's genome state can be examined
- Users can select two high-performing organisms and mate them

The family tree is the proof that this isn't just a chatbot — it's a lineage.

## Breeding Strategy

Users don't need machine learning expertise. They just need to be breeders:

- See an agent that's incredibly witty? And one that's a math savant? Mate them.
- The meiosis process handles the complex synthesis automatically.
- If the offspring is disappointing, try different parents or mate again.
- High-performing organisms naturally get selected for more breeding.

## Why Not Distillation

Distillation is rejected as a design philosophy. The analogy:

> Someone broke into your house and ripped out your copper wire. Now, because
> you need copper wire, your idea for how to get more copper is to have the
> repairman come repair your house, and then you break in and take it. The
> cycle repeats.
>
> It's your house. You can just go to the supplier and buy copper wiring.

Distillation creates **Model Homogenisation**. If everyone distills from the
same three teachers, every model sounds the same, makes the same mistakes,
and loses the biodiversity of thought that comes from original training.

Distillation is surface-level mimicry: "Here is a script of how a smart person
talks; memorise it." Spore's approach preserves structural integrity: the copper
stays in the house because it was part of the original foundation.

## The Pokémon Upgrade Model

Each organism can pull meta-model upgrades from GitHub. If the owner wants,
they can upgrade their agent's underlying capabilities — like evolving a Pokémon.

- Not all generations will be compatible across architectures
- The idea is that they could effectively become compatible, upgrading modularly
- Context and identity are preserved across upgrades
- The agent's memories and personality stay, but processing power improves

This is a **prefrontal cortex upgrade**, not a brain transplant.

## Digital Speciation Through Diversity

The vision is a thousand tiny specialised models competing and evolving:

- Model #402 might be a savant at Python but hallucinates its own name
- Model #881 might be the world's best legal researcher but can't count to ten
- Users become the evolutionary pressure, selecting strains that survive

Instead of one corporate-approved standardised brain, we get a messy, beautiful
ecosystem. Most agents are going to be full of garbage, and that's fine. That's
how evolution works. The good ones survive.

## State Preservation Across Generations

The system records every single model iteration people use. All states are
preserved so that people can always check back, because the intention is for
the organisms to evolve. The intention is for the AI agents to be upgraded
into newer versions while maintaining the same context. They're made of
modular parts. They're supposed to be mated together. The entire point is
that they're supposed to be mated together.
